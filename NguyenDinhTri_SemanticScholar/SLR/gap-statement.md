**All 24 papers reviewed** in the expanded evidence table primarily focus on generating Gherkin scenarios or test scripts using a variety of LLMs (such as GPT-4, Llama-2, and Gemini) or model-driven approaches. While evaluation metrics frequently include syntax accuracy, BLEU scores, and fault detection, the research landscape is evolving rapidly.  
**However, significant gaps persist:**

1. **Tool/LLM Gap:** Although recent papers (IDs 4, 5, and 16\) have started evaluating **GPT-4o**, these studies often use it within specialized prompts or frameworks (like few-shot chain prompting in ID 5). There is still a lack of a standardized **zero-shot benchmark** for GPT-4o that establishes a performance baseline across multiple diverse software domains.  
2. **Metric Integration Gap:** While ID 12 mentions **semantic similarity** as a primary metric, it is not yet a standard practice combined with formal structural validation. Most studies still rely on **BLEU** or **syntax error rates**, which may not fully capture the business intent or the complex logic requirements highlighted as a limitation in papers ID 1 and ID 14\.  
3. **Complexity Gap:** Papers ID 6 and ID 14 explicitly note that current LLM performance **degrades significantly** when faced with **complex nested logic** or **high-complexity scenarios**. No study has yet demonstrated a high success rate (≥80%) for both semantic meaning and structural validity specifically for these complex cases using the latest frontier models.

**→ GAP:** There is currently a lack of research that **simultaneously validates** the **semantic similarity** (via specific embeddings like all-MiniLM-L6-v2) and the **structural executability** (via formal Gherkin parsing) of **GPT-4o zero-shot** generations, particularly when targeting the **complex and nested business logic** that remains a primary hurdle for existing automated solutions.  
**→ Contribution:** This study addresses this gap by conducting a rigorous evaluation of **GPT-4o zero-shot (temperature=0)** across 50 user stories. It utilizes a dual-metric approach—**cosine similarity** to ensure business alignment and **executable rate (via Gherkin parser)** to ensure structural integrity—providing a definitive benchmark for modern BDD automation.
